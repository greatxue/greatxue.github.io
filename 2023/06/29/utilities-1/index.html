Kevin Shuey

CS Reference, Utilities

I. Preface

In the realm of computer learning, small tools play a vital role in simplifying the process and enhancing the overall experience. From Markdown to git and various other tools, these seemingly modest additions wield significant influence. They streamline tasks, facilitate collaboration, and empower learners to navigate the complex world of computer science with greater efficiency. In MIT, there is even a section teaching these technics called Missing Semester.

Markdown is a lightweight markup language used to simplify the process of formatting text. With Markdown, you can use simple syntax to add elements such as headings, lists, links, and images, without the need for complex HTML coding. It is commonly used for writing documents, blogs, notes, and more, as it offers a readable and easy-to-write format. The simplicity and usability of Markdown have made it a preferred format for many writing platforms, blogging systems, and version control tools like Git, facilitating easy sharing and dissemination of information.

Here is a quick tutorial. Hope it will help!

在计算机学习领域中，小工具扮演着简化过程和提升整体体验的重要角色。从 Markdown 到 git 以及其他各种工具，这些看似不起眼的附加物具有重大的影响力。它们简化任务、促进协作，并赋予学习者以更高效地探索复杂的计算机科学世界的能力。在 MIT, 一门特别设置的课程被用来教授这些知识，“消失的学期”。

Markdown是一种轻量级标记语言，用于简化文本格式化的过程。使用Markdown，你可以使用简单的语法标记来添加标题、列表、链接和图片等元素，而无需繁琐的HTML代码。它适用于编写文档、博客、笔记等，具有易读易写的特点。Markdown的简洁性和易用性使得它成为许多写作平台、博客系统和版本控制工具（如Git）的首选格式，方便了信息的共享和传播。

祝你学得愉快！

II. Tutorial

1 Basic Grammar

1.1 Topics

The topic is marked by #, with smaller fonts as numbers of # increase.

1.2 Fonts

The font-types are marked by either * or _ .

  • Shortcuts:
    Bold: cmd+B
    Italics: cmd+I

1.3 Newline

There are many ways in mdarkdown to start a new line:

  • Double space after a sentence.
  • Spare a blank line between sentences.
  • Add a <br/> in between.

1.4 Quote

Quote is realized with symbol >.

  • The space after is optional.
  • Newline is allowed in the quote, and a blank line is needed to end the quote.
  • Mutiple use of symbols is allowed.

1.7 Lists

Lists could be divided into ordered and unordered lists.

  • Ordered lists could be realized using *,+,-, followed by a space.
  • Unordered lists could be realized using 1., 2., also followed by a space.
  • To control the level of lists, using tab.

Example:

  • List 1
  • List 2
    • List 2.1
      • List 2.1.1
    • List 2.2
